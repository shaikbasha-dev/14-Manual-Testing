# Scrum Terminology

## Introduction

The **Scrum Framework** uses several important terms that help the Scrum Team plan, manage, and deliver software efficiently. Understanding these terms is essential for developers, testers, Scrum Masters, and Product Owners because they are frequently used during Agile software development.

This document explains the most commonly used Scrum terminologies along with their purpose and real-world examples.

---

## 1. User Story

### Definition

A **User Story** is a **feature or module** in an application or software that represents a specific functionality required by the customer.

A User Story describes a small piece of work that provides value to the end user.

### Characteristics

- **Represents a single functionality.**
- **Easy to understand.**
- **Customer-focused.**
- **Can be completed within a Sprint.**

### Example

For an **Online Shopping Application**, the following can be considered User Stories:

- User Registration
- User Login
- Product Search
- Add to Cart
- Online Payment
- Order History

Each feature is treated as an individual **User Story**.

---

## 2. Epic

### Definition

An **Epic** is a collection of multiple **User Stories** that together represent a major functionality or a complete business feature.

Large projects are divided into Epics, and each Epic is further divided into smaller User Stories.

### Example

**Epic:** Online Shopping System

User Stories under this Epic:

- User Registration
- User Login
- Product Search
- Shopping Cart
- Payment Gateway
- Order Tracking

---

## 3. Product Backlog

### Definition

The **Product Backlog** is a prioritized collection of all User Stories prepared and maintained by the **Product Owner**.

It contains every feature, enhancement, bug fix, and improvement that needs to be developed in the project.

### Characteristics

- **Prepared by the Product Owner.**
- **Contains all User Stories.**
- **Continuously updated.**
- **Prioritized according to business value.**

### Example

A Product Backlog may include:

- User Registration
- Login
- Forgot Password
- Product Search
- Shopping Cart
- Payment Module
- Order History

---

## 4. Sprint

### Definition

A **Sprint** is a fixed period during which the Scrum Team completes the selected User Stories from the Product Backlog.

The Sprint duration is decided by the **Product Owner** and the Scrum Team.

Usually, a Sprint lasts between **1 and 4 weeks**.

### Characteristics

- **Fixed time period.**
- **Delivers working software.**
- **Focused on selected User Stories.**
- **Ends with a potentially shippable product increment.**

### Example

If a Sprint duration is **2 weeks**, the Scrum Team may complete:

- User Registration
- Login
- Product Search

before the Sprint ends.

---

## 5. Sprint Planning (Kickoff Meeting)

### Definition

**Sprint Planning**, also known as the **Kickoff Meeting**, is conducted before the beginning of every Sprint.

During this meeting, the Scrum Team decides what work can realistically be completed during the upcoming Sprint.

### Objectives

- **Select User Stories from the Product Backlog.**
- **Define Sprint Goals.**
- **Estimate work effort.**
- **Assign responsibilities.**
- **Prepare the Sprint Backlog.**

---

## 6. Sprint Backlog

### Definition

The **Sprint Backlog** is the collection of User Stories that the **Developers** and **Testers** commit to completing during a specific Sprint.

Unlike the Product Backlog, it contains only the work planned for the current Sprint.

### Characteristics

- **Created during Sprint Planning.**
- **Contains committed User Stories.**
- **Managed by the Scrum Team.**
- **Updated throughout the Sprint.**

### Example

Sprint Backlog for Sprint 1:

- User Registration
- Login
- Forgot Password

---

## 7. Scrum Meeting (Daily Stand-up Meeting)

### Definition

The **Scrum Meeting**, also known as the **Daily Stand-up Meeting**, is conducted every day by the **Scrum Master**.

The meeting usually lasts **15 minutes**.

Its purpose is to monitor Sprint progress and identify any issues that may affect the team's work.

### Questions Discussed

Each team member answers the following three questions:

**1. What did you do yesterday?**

**2. What will you do today?**

**3. Are there any obstacles or blockers in your work?**

### Benefits

- **Improves team communication.**
- **Tracks daily progress.**
- **Identifies obstacles quickly.**
- **Helps achieve Sprint goals on time.**

---

## 8. Burn-up Chart / Burn-down Chart

### Definition

A **Burn-up Chart** or **Burn-down Chart** is maintained daily by the **Scrum Master** to monitor Sprint progress.

It helps the Scrum Team understand:

- **Completed work**
- **Remaining work**
- **Sprint progress**
- **Likelihood of completing the Sprint on time**

### Burn-down Chart

Shows the **remaining work** throughout the Sprint.

As work is completed, the remaining work decreases until it reaches zero.

### Burn-up Chart

Shows the **completed work** throughout the Sprint.

As development progresses, the completed work increases until the Sprint goal is achieved.

### Benefits

- **Measures Sprint progress.**
- **Improves project tracking.**
- **Helps identify delays early.**
- **Supports better Sprint planning.**

---

## 9. Sprint Retrospective Meeting

### Definition

The **Sprint Retrospective Meeting** is conducted after the completion of a Sprint.

The purpose of this meeting is to review the completed Sprint and identify opportunities for improvement before starting the next Sprint.

### Discussion Points

The Scrum Team discusses:

- **What went well?**
- **What problems were faced?**
- **What can be improved?**
- **How can the next Sprint be more effective?**

### Benefits

- **Encourages continuous improvement.**
- **Improves team collaboration.**
- **Helps prevent repeated mistakes.**
- **Enhances overall Sprint performance.**

```text
        │
        ▼

        │
        ▼
Sprint Backlog
        │
        ▼

        │

        │
        ▼

        │
        ▼
Sprint Retrospective
        │
        ▼

```

---

## Key Points

- **User Story represents a single feature or functionality.**
- **Epic is a collection of related User Stories.**
- **The Product Backlog is maintained by the Product Owner.**
- **A Sprint usually lasts between 1 and 4 weeks.**
- **Sprint Planning defines the work for the upcoming Sprint.**
- **Sprint Backlog contains committed User Stories for a Sprint.**
- **Daily Scrum Meetings are conducted for 15 minutes every day.**
- **Burn-up and Burn-down Charts help monitor Sprint progress.**
- **Sprint Retrospective focuses on continuous improvement.**

---

## Summary

**Scrum Terminology** forms the foundation of the Scrum Framework and Agile software development. Concepts such as **User Stories**, **Epics**, **Product Backlog**, **Sprint**, **Sprint Planning**, **Sprint Backlog**, **Daily Scrum Meetings**, **Burn-up/Burn-down Charts**, and **Sprint Retrospectives** help Scrum Teams organize work, improve collaboration, monitor progress, and continuously deliver high-quality software. Understanding these terms is essential for every Agile team member and is frequently tested in software testing and Scrum interviews.
